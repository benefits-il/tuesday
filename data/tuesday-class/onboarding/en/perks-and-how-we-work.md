Tuesday — perks and how we operate

Not the employment contract, you already signed that. These are the small things that make the difference day to day, and how we actually work.

Hours and flexibility
There's no time clock. We measure results, not hours. Most of the team starts somewhere between nine and ten and finishes when the work is done, not when the clock hits a certain number. Need to step out in the middle of the day for an errand, go. Just make sure whoever depends on you knows.

Working from home
Two days a week from home is the default, and you can arrange otherwise as needed. We're a company that built a product for people who work alone, so the idea that you can work well outside the office doesn't confuse us. What matters is that you're reachable when needed and that things get done.

Meetings
As few as possible. If something can be solved in a message, it'll be solved in a message. There's one short weekly team meeting, and that's pretty much it. We save your time for the real work.

The small things
Genuinely good coffee, not just the kitchenette stuff, Dana will show you where. A shared lunch on the day of the weekly meeting. A personal learning budget for courses and books, because we expect you to keep learning. A day off on your birthday, because why not.

Vacation
You take what you need, responsibly. No obsessive day-counting. A person who never takes time off is a problem, not an achievement. Arrange it ahead, take it, come back.
