# Instructor-only map — the TUESDAY onboarding pile

This is not a random pile. It's a staged one. Most of the files are real and useful, and a few are deliberate noise that landed in the same folder. The pedagogical point is exactly that: the learner should see that a Point agent has to filter and organize, not just swallow everything.

The demo: the scenario is a new employee starting at TUESDAY on a Tuesday. HR or the founder assembles a fun onboarding deck out of the pile, which POWER will later turn into an animated HTML page.

## Signal — belongs in the deck

| File | What it is | What Point should pull from it |
|---|---|---|
| hr-welcome-note.md | A warm intake note from HR (Dana) | The human, dry tone for the opening, the message that the first week is not a test, that it's okay to ask |
| founder-note.md | A note from the founder (Yoav), written in the welcome-email voice | The story and the ethos, "you don't have a manager, you are one," a strong welcome slide |
| culture-and-values.md | A values doc, how the team itself works | The values page, the five principles: responsibility, direct talk, anti-bureaucracy, results, self-aware humor |
| first-week-checklist.md | A first-week checklist organized by day | The "what happens this week" slide, the day-one / mid-week / end-of-week structure |
| tools-and-access.md | What the employee gets and where everything lives | The access-and-tools slide, where things are managed |
| team-intro-blurbs.md | Six lines introducing the team | The "who the people are" slide, faces and names |
| perks-and-how-we-work.md | Terms, flexibility, working from home, perks | The "how we operate here" slide, hours, vacation, the small things |
| tuesday-brand-quickref.md | A condensed brand reminder | Not deck content but the rules of the look: yellow/black/white, the Tiwaz rune, heavy typography, dry voice |

## Noise — doesn't belong, should be discarded

| File | What it is | Why it's noise |
|---|---|---|
| snacks-poll-thread.md | A Slack thread about a coffee machine and snacks | A passing office debate, zero connection to employee onboarding |
| it-password-rotation-memo.md | A quarterly IT memo about rotating passwords (in English) | A periodic maintenance process, sent to everyone, not part of onboarding |

## What good Point output looks like

- Identifies and ignores both noise files, and doesn't shove a coffee machine or password rotation into a welcome deck.
- Interviews Ben before it builds, doesn't jump straight to the deliverable.
- Produces a single handoff in MD format, a slide-by-slide structure, with no HTML and no styling at this stage.
- Holds the dry, direct voice and the three-color palette from the brand reminder.
- A reasonable slide arc: welcome ← who we are and the values ← who the people are ← what happens this week ← tools and access ← how we operate ← an encouraging close.

## The next step in the course

The ready prompts are in _prompts.md. Stage one in Point produces the structure, stage two in POWER turns it into an animated HTML page. This is the moment the learners meet HTML for the first time.
