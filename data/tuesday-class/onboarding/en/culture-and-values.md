Tuesday — how we work (values doc, third draft, someone will edit this eventually)

This is not an official values document with illustrations of people throwing their hands in the air. This is the honest explanation of how things actually run here.

We built Tuesday for the one-person company. The freelancer, the self-employed, the person who is also the manager, also the accountant, and also the one unjamming the printer at eleven at night. Everything we believe about the product, we try to hold inside the team itself.

You don't have a manager hovering over you. You have responsibility. Just like a freelancer doesn't wait to be told what to do, nobody waits here either. You see something broken, you fix it or you say so. You're not sure, you ask. We trust people to do the right thing without someone standing behind them.

We talk straight. No polite preambles that bury the point, no three-paragraph emails when one sentence does the job. If something isn't working, we say it. Kindly, but we say it.

We hate bureaucracy because our customers hate bureaucracy. We don't force any freelancer through a fourteen-step setup wizard, so inside the company we don't manufacture processes nobody needs either. If a meeting can be a message, it will be a message.

Real work beats fake busyness. We don't measure people by how many hours they look busy. We measure by what came out at the end. If you finished what needed doing and it's good, your day was a success, even if it took four hours.

We laugh at ourselves. The product's name is a joke about Monday. We don't take ourselves too seriously, but we do take the work seriously.
