Tuesday — welcome note from HR

From: Dana, People and Ops
Sent Sunday evening, the night before your first day

Hi,

So you got the job. Four conversations, half the team met, and you've probably already forgotten most of the names. That's completely fine. Nobody here remembers names on the first day, except me, and that's my job.

Here's what matters before tomorrow. We're a small company. There's no onboarding department with three weeks of mentoring and a two-hundred-page handbook. There's this note, there's the team, and there are plenty of questions you're allowed and encouraged to ask. People who don't ask here get stuck. People who quietly stay stuck for a week, we find out too late. Don't be that person.

Tomorrow morning, don't show up with first-day-of-work dread. We built an entire product for people who work alone, so we know exactly how lonely it feels when nobody tells you what to do. You won't be alone here. You'll have access to everyone you need, and to me, and to the genuinely good coffee, not the one in the kitchenette.

The only thing I'm asking of you for day one is to show up, open your laptop, and not try to understand everything at once. We take it slow. The first week is not a test.

Welcome to Tuesday. We're glad you're here.

Dana
