# The ask — how much and what for

## How much

We're raising a Seed round of 1.2 million dollars. That should carry us about 18 months and bring us to a clear target: cross 50,000 dollars MRR and bring monthly churn below 3 percent. If we get there, a Series A is a much easier conversation.

## Where the money goes

I split it roughly like this, not locked down to the dollar:

- Product and engineering, around 45 percent. Two additional engineering hires. Most of the near-term work is around Tasks 2.0 and the Customer Portal, the two things at the heart of our differentiation, and they need to be strong.
- Marketing and growth, around 30 percent. Until now growth has been almost entirely organic. This money is to test whether growth can be bought efficiently, without burning cash. Start small and measure.
- Retention and customer success, around 15 percent. Churn is too high and it's our biggest risk. One hire whose entire job is to understand why people leave and stop it.
- Operations and reserve, around 10 percent. Infrastructure, tools, and a safety cushion.

## Why now and not later

We have a live product, paying customers, and organic growth. What's missing is fuel. We got this far with almost no money, which means the unit economics are healthy. This money is to accelerate something that already works, not to test whether it works.

The number to remember: 1.2 million dollars, for 18 months, to reach 50,000 MRR and break the churn problem.
