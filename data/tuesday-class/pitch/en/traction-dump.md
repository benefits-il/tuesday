# Numbers — what I've got so far (messy)

This isn't a clean report. These are things I pulled from the analytics and the billing system, written down as I went. The tidy numbers are in the table in metrics.csv; this is the explanation and the context.

We launched the public version in September. Since then, nice growth but not smooth — there was one month (February) where we barely grew at all, probably because of a signup bug we caught too late.

Cumulative signups crossed 9,000 or so around the middle of May. Most of them on the free plan, which is expected in this model. Of those, the paying users are on the order of 600-plus, meaning free-to-paid conversion around 7 percent, which isn't bad for the category.

MRR as of the end of May stands at something like 11,400 dollars. At the start of the year we were under 4,000, so we roughly tripled in five months. ARPU comes out low, around 18 to 19 dollars per paying user, which makes sense because most of the payers are on the cheap plan.

Monthly churn of paying users runs between 4 and 6 percent. That's high. I know it's high. A freelancer who has a weak month just cancels. We're working on it; part of the fix is connecting tasks to money, so people feel they're losing something if they leave.

Retention after 3 months, out of everyone who ever paid, around 70 percent. The first month is the critical one — whoever gets through it usually stays.

Visit-to-signup conversion runs around 3 to 4 percent, which is healthy for a landing page with no sales calls.

One more thing worth noting: most of the growth is organic, word of mouth and a bit of content. We've spent almost nothing on paid ads. That means there's something here that people talk about, but also that we haven't yet tested whether growth can be bought with money.

To check before the deck: the exact number of paying users, and April's churn — I think it dropped a little and I want to confirm.
