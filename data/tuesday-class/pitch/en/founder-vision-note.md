# Why I'm even building this (for myself, thoughts)

This isn't an official document. I wrote it at night after a call with an investor who told me "what's the story." So here's the story, the way it comes out of me.

I was a freelancer for seven years. Design, mostly. And I'm telling you, the hard part of being independent isn't the work. The work I love, that's why I left in the first place. The hard part is everything around it. The invoices. The client who doesn't answer and then answers at five in the morning. The "wait, what was I supposed to do today." The feeling that you're constantly chasing your own life instead of running it.

I tried everything. Monday, too big for me. Notion, I built myself a gorgeous system that took me two weeks and then I never touched it. Excel, of course, like everyone. In the end I went back to sticky notes on the desk. A grown adult with sticky notes on the desk.

And then I thought, wait — all these tools were built for companies. All of them. They talk about a team, about assigning, about collaboration. And I'm alone. I'm the manager and the worker and the whole department. There's no tool built for me. They were all built for someone with a team, and I'm trying to shove myself into it.

That's the idea behind Tuesday. Not another management tool. A tool that understands you're alone, and that it isn't a bug, it's the whole point. That Monday is for Monday, the day of companies and meetings and employees. And you, the freelancer, you've been fighting since Tuesday. It's named after Tyr, the Norse god who did justice entirely on his own, no committees. Sounds about right to me.

(By the way, my sister works at NVIDIA and told me that there too, even in a giant company, everyone ends up feeling alone with their own tasks. So maybe it isn't only freelancers. But that's not my crowd, I'm not going there, I just noted it because it connected.)

What I want people to feel when they open Tuesday isn't "wow, so many features." I want them to feel "finally, someone built this for me." That's it. That's the whole vision. That the person building a business alone feels like they've got someone at their back.

And to the investor who said "what's the story," here it is. The story is that there are a million human beings sitting alone in front of a screen trying to run a whole business, and everyone handed them tools built for companies. We're the first to stop and ask what someone who's alone actually needs. That's the market, and it's also the right thing to do.
