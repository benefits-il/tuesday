# Pricing — current state (snapshot, May)

This is the pricing as it lives on the site right now. We might change it after the round, but this is what we've got.

## The plans

Free. No credit card. Up to 3 active clients, basic tasks, no Customer Portal. This is the plan most people come in through. Its goal is to let you actually work, not to nag you until you upgrade.

Solo, 19 dollars a month. Unlimited clients, the full Tasks 2.0, invoices. This is the main plan, most of the payers are here.

Solo Plus, 39 dollars a month. Everything in Solo, plus the Customer Portal, plus automatic reminders to clients who are late paying. This is the plan we push toward, because the portal is the thing that makes people stay.

Annual payment gives roughly two months free, comes out to a discount on the order of 17 percent.

## What I see in the numbers

Most payers are on Solo, which is why ARPU is low, around 18 to 19 dollars. The clear opportunity is to move people from Solo up to Solo Plus, because the portal both lowers churn and raises revenue. Two birds.

What I haven't touched yet: pricing for more expensive markets, and a pay-per-use option for people who work seasonally. Noted for the future.

Note: don't present the exact prices as the target, present the direction — raise ARPU through the portal. The investor cares about the direction, not the number.
