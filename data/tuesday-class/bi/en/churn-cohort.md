# Churn — Cohort Breakdown (draft, not cleaned up)

Pulled this out of the database in a quick run, didn't have time to clean it. Most of the data is here. A few rows are missing a reason because the customer just stopped paying and never answered the exit survey.

## Monthly churn (percent of paying subscribers)
- October: 4.1%
- November: 4.0%
- December: 3.8%
- January: 3.6%
- February: 3.9%
- March: 4.4%
- April: 4.8%
- May: 5.1%

Churn came down nicely through January and then started climbing. May is the worst we've ever had.

## Who is leaving
Most of the people leaving are relatively new subscribers — signed up in the last three months and left. Less churn among the veterans. So we're losing people early, not after two years.

## Cut by usage
People who never touched "Tasks 2.0" at all churn at a higher rate than people who did use it. But — and this is where it starts to get interesting — out of the people who did adopt "Tasks 2.0," the ones who tried the feature and then abandoned it after two weeks churn out of the whole product at the highest rate of anyone. In other words, the people who tried the feature and were let down are the ones who left.

## Exit reasons (from the exit survey, the people who bothered to answer)
- "Too complicated, I expected something simple" — 31%
- "Found a cheaper tool" — 22%
- "Didn't use it enough to justify the cost" — 19%
- "Tasks 2.0 didn't work the way I expected" — 14%
- "Switched to Notion" — 9%
- No answer / unclear reason — about a fifth of the leavers, didn't fold them into the percentages above
