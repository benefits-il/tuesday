# Competitor Moves — What We Caught This Quarter

An informal collection from blogs, release notes, and freelancer groups. Not all of it is verified, but the direction is clear.

## Monday
- They launched a discounted "Solo" tier aimed directly at independents. That is our territory. Until now Monday was only about teams.
- They added an AI assistant that breaks a project into tasks automatically. Exactly what "Tasks 2.0" tried to do, except they made it smoother.

## Notion
- They pushed hard on freelancer templates and on automations.
- They added a basic customer portal. That is our strong side, and now they have something there.
- They are still seen as "too complicated" by many freelancers. That is our opening.

## Small and new tools
- Two or three very cheap, freelancer-focused tools have popped up that do one thing well — time tracking and invoicing. They are taking customers from the cheap end.

## What this means
The market we play in suddenly got crowded. Monday is coming in from below, Notion is coming in on the portal, and cheap tools are nibbling at the edge. But — none of them is yet "simple and understands freelancers" the way we are perceived. Our differentiator is the simplicity, not the heavy features. It is telling that "Tasks 2.0" pushed us in exactly the direction where the competitors are stronger.
