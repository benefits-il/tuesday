# Revenue and Pricing Snapshot — Brief

As of end of May. Numbers rounded, enough for the decision.

## Revenue
- Monthly recurring revenue (MRR): about $148K.
- MRR growth in the last quarter: about 2% only. Almost flat. Last year we grew 6%–8% per quarter.
- The slowdown in growth started roughly when churn started to climb. Probably related.

## Pricing
- A free plan, then a subscription at about $12 a month.
- "Tasks 2.0" is included in the existing subscription. We never charged extra for it. In other words it brings in no direct revenue — it was sold as something that would retain and attract subscribers.

## Operating cost of "Tasks 2.0"
- Two senior developers are assigned to it full time. That is our most expensive team.
- The AI infrastructure that runs it costs real money per call. The more people use it, the more it costs. Right now that is about $9,000 a month in compute costs, and it grows with adoption.
- In other words: the feature costs us more the more it is used, but it does not charge any additional money on its own.

## How to read this
"Tasks 2.0" is a cost center, not a profit center. Its only justification is if it retains subscribers or attracts new ones. The numbers to cross-check are exactly those: whether it actually reduces churn or increases adoption enough to justify its cost. If not — it drains two expensive developers and a growing compute budget with nothing in return.
