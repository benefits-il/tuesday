# Carousel outline (input for /carousel)

Content for a five-slide Instagram carousel. Just the words and the order, not the graphics. The plugin handles the visual construction; this is what the slides say.

## Topic
"You are the whole company." The solo operator wearing every hat at once, and what Tuesday actually removes from the pile.

## Layout reference (one line)
Five square slides, identical visual style: near-black background, one big yellow line of heavy uppercase type per slide, the yellow Tiwaz up-arrow mark small in the corner.

## Slide 1: Hook
Headline: "YOU DON'T HAVE A MANAGER."
Sub: "You are one. And the accountant. And the guy who fixes the printer."

## Slide 2: The problem
Headline: "SO EVERYTHING LIVES EVERYWHERE."
Sub: "Clients in your inbox. Invoices in a spreadsheet. The actual plan in your head, mostly."

## Slide 3: Point one
Headline: "ONE PLACE FOR CLIENTS."
Sub: "Who they are, what is open, what they owe. No more digging through email."

## Slide 4: Point two
Headline: "ONE PLACE FOR MONEY."
Sub: "Invoices sent, invoices paid, invoices pretending they didn't see it. Tuesday keeps score."

## Slide 5: Close
Headline: "START TUESDAY."
Sub: "Free. No card, no demo call. Just the button."

## Tone cue
Each slide is a single clean punch. Read top to bottom, it tells a brief story: you are overloaded, your data is scattered, here is the fix, here is the entry point. No buzzwords, no exclamation marks.
