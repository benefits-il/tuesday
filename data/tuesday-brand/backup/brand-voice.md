# Tuesday — Brand Voice Guidelines

> Backup file. This is what station 1 produces when you run `brand-voice` on the Tuesday raw materials. Use it ONLY if you got stuck in station 1 and need to move on to station 2. The real exercise is to generate your own from the `raw/` materials — that is the whole point of the chain.

Distilled from Tuesday's own materials: the welcome email, the social posts, the landing hero, and the press boilerplate. The corporate "feature announcement" and the un-fixed draft were read as counter-examples — what Tuesday sounds like when it loses its voice.

## Who Tuesday talks to

The solo operator. One person running the whole business: the work, the clients, the invoices, and the printer that just jammed. They are not a "user" and not a "valued customer." They are a peer who knows the grind. Tuesday talks across the table, never down from a stage.

## How Tuesday sounds

Dry, direct, a little self-aware. The humor is in the truth, not in the joke. Tuesday states the real thing plainly and trusts the reader to get it. Warm when it earns it, never gushing.

The register shifts slightly by context: outreach is cooler and more careful, a welcome email is a notch warmer (one genuine, quiet bit of warmth is allowed when the warmth is real). The spine stays the same everywhere.

## Do

- **Open from the side.** Start with a small true scene of the solo grind, not with the product. "Staring at a notes file called URGENT URGENT" beats "We're excited to introduce."
- **Say what Tuesday does, never what it'll transform.** It holds your clients, projects, and invoices in one place. It won't do the work. That honesty is the pitch.
- **One parenthetical aside that does real work.** A dry side-comment that lands. ("Nothing does the work for you. We checked.") One per piece, not three.
- **Land one clear message, then stop.** Short. Most pieces stay under 130 words. End on a line that stamps it.
- **Give exactly one next step.** "Add your first client. There's no fourteen-step wizard. There's a button. You'll find it."

## Don't

- No exclamation marks (a single quiet one is allowed only in a warm welcome, only if the warmth is real).
- No buzzwords: no "leverage," "seamless," "elevate," "unlock," "10x," "game-changer," "journey," "next level."
- No emojis. No em-dashes. No hashtag soup (one or two plain hashtags at most, if any).
- No "team of experts," no "we're thrilled," no fake urgency ("don't miss out").
- No selling to someone who already said yes. After signup, drop the pitch.

## Signature lines (the brand's own)

- Monday is for companies. Tuesday is for the one-person company.
- You don't have a manager. You are one.
- Start free. No card, no sales call.
- The salaried crowd starts on Monday. You've been fighting since Tuesday.

## Before and after

**Off-voice (what to rescue it from):**
> We are excited to introduce our new Client Portal, a powerful feature that enables users to enhance collaboration and elevate the client experience.

**On-voice:**
> New: a client portal. Your clients get one place to see what's done, what's next, and what they owe — instead of three forwarded emails and a "hey, quick question." It's live now. Turn it on per client.

The fix is not a thesaurus pass. It is the same fact told by a peer who respects the reader's time.
