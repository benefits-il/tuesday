# Character brief (input for /reel)

Specifications for a short AI-character video. Includes the character outline, a 15-second script, and brand-style notes.

## The character
Tess. Mid-thirties. Runs a solo design studio from a corner of her home. Dry, unbothered. She has the look of someone who has sent one too many reminder emails. Unpolished, not a stock model. A real freelancer at 4 PM. Dark utilitarian clothes, with one small Tuesday yellow detail (a mug, a sticky note, a pin) so she fits the brand without wearing a costume.

## The setting
Her desk. A near-black room, one warm desk lamp, a laptop. The yellow Tiwaz mark is visible but small (a sticker on the laptop lid, a poster edge). Lived-in, quiet, realistic.

## The 15-second script (spoken to camera)
- (0-4s) "I don't have a manager. People keep congratulating me on that."
- (5-9s) "They have clearly never been the manager, the accountant, and IT support before lunch."
- (10-15s) "Tuesday keeps the clients and the invoices in one place. So I can go back to the part I actually went freelance for."

## Brand-style notes
Black and yellow, geometric, clean. Tess delivers her lines flat and a little tired. The joke is in the truth, not the performance. End with the yellow Tiwaz mark filling the frame. No music swells, no captions shouting, no exclamation marks. It must feel like a real person, not a commercial.
