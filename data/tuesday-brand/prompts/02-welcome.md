# /welcome (new-user welcome email, saved as a Gmail draft)

Paste this into Cowork with the Tuesday plugin installed and the brand pack attached. It writes the welcome email a brand-new user gets the moment they sign up, and saves it to your Gmail drafts.

---

```
/welcome

You are writing the welcome email a brand-new Tuesday user gets the minute they create an account. Tuesday is the work-management app for solo people running the whole show.

Read these attached files before writing:
- brand-voice.md: the Tuesday voice guidelines you generated in station 1. Hold it the whole time.
- brand-sheet.md: Tuesday's facts, colors, and mark.
- welcome-points.md: what to welcome them about, the one first step, the tone cue, and what NOT to promise.

Write the email so it:
- Opens warm but dry. They already said yes, so do not sell. A line that meets them where they are (staring at an empty workspace), not "we're thrilled to have you on this journey."
- Names the real win from welcome-points.md without a parade: they moved their business out of the three-apps-and-a-notes-file life.
- Gives exactly ONE first step: add your first client. One client. Make clear there's no fourteen-step wizard, there's a button, they'll find it.
- Says what Tuesday does, never what it'll transform. No "10x," no "unlock," no "exciting journey."
- Uses Tuesday's voice from brand-voice.md: peer to peer, one parenthetical aside doing real work, no buzzwords, no emojis, no em-dashes. This register is a notch warmer than outreach, so one genuine, quiet exclamation is allowed only if the warmth is real.
- Stays short, under 130 words, and closes on a human line from a person, not "the team of experts."
- Subject line: plain and a little dry, the kind you'd actually open.

Then save it as a Gmail draft. Do not send it. Tell me it's ready and show me the subject line and body so I can read it first.
```
