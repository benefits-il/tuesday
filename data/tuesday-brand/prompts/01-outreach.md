# /outreach (cold outreach email, saved as a Gmail draft)

Paste this into Cowork with the Tuesday plugin installed and the brand pack attached. It runs the content-writer and saves the result straight to your Gmail drafts. Swap the placeholder in parentheses for your recipient details.

---

```
/outreach

You are writing one short cold outreach email for Tuesday, the work-management app for solo people running the whole show.

Read these attached files before writing:
- brand-voice.md: the Tuesday voice guidelines you generated in station 1. This is the law for how it sounds.
- brand-sheet.md: Tuesday's facts, colors, and mark.
- outreach-context.md: who this email is for, the ask, and the one key message it must carry.
- raw/feature-announcement.md: the "Tasks 2.0" corporate blast. Read it ONLY to pull out the one real, useful fact buried in the jargon. Do not copy a word of its tone. That tone is exactly what you are rescuing this message from.

Recipient: (replace with name, role, and how they found us, e.g. "Maya, freelance brand designer, found us via a newsletter").

Write the email so it:
- Opens from the side, not on the product. A relation, a small true scene of the solo grind, never "I wanted to reach out about Tuesday."
- Lands the one key message from outreach-context.md plainly: clients, projects, invoices in one place, it won't do the work, it stops you losing the thread, start free with no card and no demo call.
- Carries Tuesday's voice from brand-voice.md: dry, direct, peer to peer, a little self-aware. Use one parenthetical aside that does real work. No buzzwords, no exclamation marks, no emojis, no em-dashes.
- Stays short. Under 130 words. A subject line that earns the open without hype.
- Closes on a line that lands and a single clear reason to click, not a "don't miss out."

Then save it as a Gmail draft addressed to the recipient. Do not send it. Tell me the draft is ready and show me the subject line and body so I can read it before it goes anywhere.
```
